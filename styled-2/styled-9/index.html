  • A short introduction on Gravitational Waves in Farsi

Gravitational Waves are one of the solid predictions of Einstein's theory of General Relativity. They are ripples in the fabric of spacetime that has physical, measurable effects that have not been detected yet. These effects are extremely small. The sources of Gravitational Waves, that our detectors on Earth (and space, in future) are sensitive to, are violent celestial events such as coalesce of two huge galaxies far, far away. Detecting and analyzing the Gravitational Wave signals will soon open a new, exciting area of Astronomy in Gravitational Wave spectrum beyond the traditional Astronomy in electromagnetic spectrum. There are many resources available online giving brief introduction to Gravitational Waves, however I wrote one in Farsi available here.
