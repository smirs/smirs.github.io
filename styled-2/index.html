My name is Saeed Mirshekari. I am a Physicist by training and a Data Scientist by trade. I've lived in 6 cities in 4 different countries in 4 continents. I was born and grew up in Tehran, Iran (1983-2008). I have lived in St. Louis MO (2009-2012), Gainesville FL (2012), Milwaukee WI (2013) in the United States, in Paris, France (2012) and in São Paulo, Brazil (2013-2015). I currently live in St. Louis, MO.

I received my pre-college and college education in Tehran, obtaining a B.Sc. degree in Physics from University of Tehran in 2005. I continued my higher education at University of Tehran as a graduate student in Gravitation and Astrophysics. During my Master program, I worked on fundamental General Relativity and defended my thesis in February 2008. In the same year, I started a PhD program at Washington University in St. Louis under the supervision of Distinguished Professor of Physics, Clifford Will. I defended my dissertation on Gravitational Waves and Alternative Theories of Gravity in May 2013.

Right after my PhD I was awarded a fellowship by the top Brazilian science funding agency, FAPESP, as a Postdoctoral Research Fellow at the International Center for Theoretical Physics in São Paulo, Brazil, ICTP-SAIFR (2013-2015). During this period of time, I was also a researcher in the USA based LIGO Scientific Collaboration. During my career in academia, I also worked as Visiting Research Scholar at several universities and research institutes including: Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT) (March 2011), IAP, Paris (Jun - Sep 2012), and University of Florida (Nov 2012 - Aug 2013), and California Institute of Technology (Caltech) (Mar 2015).

I started my industrial career as Data Scientist in late 2015. I am an ENTJ.
